NZBget Setup Guide

This guide will walk you through the steps to get nzb360 connected to NZBget running on your computer. If you haven't already, download and install NZBget on your computer and configure it to connect to your usenet provider.

Step 1: Primary Connection Address

The first step in connecting nzb360 to NZBget is filling in your primary connection address into nzb360's NZBget settings, shown below.

You can find your primary connection address by looking at the URL in your browser on your PC when NZBget is running.

If your URL shows http://127.0.0.1:6789 or http://localhost:6789 that is not accurate, as it is referring to your localhost address. You will need to find your private IP address for your computer running NZBget. Follow this guide to find your PRIVATE IP address.

The :6789 is the port number NZBget is running on. By default, NZBget uses port 6789. If you haven't changed it, then :6789 is probably what you'll want to use to start.

If you're using a remote address (not a local IP address) then you'll want to use that in your Primary Connection Address.

Once you've entered in your Primary Connection Address, move on to step 2.

Step 2: Adding your User/Password

NZBget is secured via a username and password, which you must enter into nzb360 to connect to NZBget. You can find your username and password by launching NZBget and clicking on Settings -> Security.

Look for ControlUsername and ControlPassword. Type those two values into nzb360's NZBget Username and Password fields (shown below).

Step 3: Test your connection

nzb360 allows you to test your connection from the settings screen to see if things will work.

Note: Your mobile device MUST be on the same network as NZBget (if you're using a local connection, i.e. 192.168.x.x). Make sure your mobile device is on your home wifi.

Press the Test Connection button.

  • If you get a Success you're all set!
  • If you get a Failed to Connect follow the troubleshooting tips below.

Troubleshooting

  • Make sure your Primary Connection String is correct.
  • Double check your username and password.
  • Make sure no firewall rules are blocking external access.
  • Make sure you're on the same network and are connected via wifi on your mobile device.

Tips and Tricks

  • Did you know you can tap and hold on the Pause button to individually pause NZBget's services?
  • Did you know you can tap on the Server Info (underneath the 3 dot menu) to quickly access the server info pane?
